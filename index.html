Our Mission

Bridging the Clinical Lab and the Bedside

At the Chin-Yee & Hsia Hematology Research Lab, we connect laboratory insights with real-world clinical decisions to transform care for patients with blood disorders.

Central to this work is fostering clear communication between laboratory and clinical teams — ensuring new evidence, diagnostic reports, and biomarkers are translated into timely, evidence-based decisions.

"We bridge insights between the clinical laboratory and the bedside to transform patient care."
— Lab Mission Statement

Research Focus

Advancing the Science of Blood Disorders

Our work spans three core pillars — early disease identification, AI-guided decision support, and understanding how clonal disease evolves over time.

01

Early Identification of Rare Disease

We investigate how subtle laboratory signals can serve as early warning signs for rare hematologic conditions like PNH and immune cytopenias, enabling faster diagnosis and intervention.

02

AI-Driven Clinical Decision Support

By leveraging machine learning on longitudinal real-world patient data, we develop predictive models that help clinicians make evidence-based decisions with greater precision.

03

Clonal Evolution in PNH

We study how clonal evolution in paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria shapes disease trajectories over time — linking laboratory changes with clinical outcomes.
