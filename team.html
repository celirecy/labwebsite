Leadership

Principal Investigators

Our lab is co-led by two clinician-scientists with complementary expertise spanning clinical hematology, laboratory medicine, and translational research.

Dr. Benjamin Chin-Yee

Principal Investigator ¬∑ Hematologist

Dr. Chin-Yee is a hematologist and clinician-scientist whose research focuses on the early identification of rare hematologic diseases and the use of novel biomarkers to guide clinical decision-making.

His work bridges the clinical laboratory and patient care, aiming to reduce diagnostic delays and improve outcomes for patients with rare blood disorders ‚Äî including PNH, immune cytopenias, and unexplained cytopenia syndromes.

Dr. Chin-Yee is also committed to training the next generation of physician-scientists, mentoring students and fellows in both clinical and research settings.

Dr. Cyrus Hsia

Principal Investigator ¬∑ Hematologist

Dr. Hsia is a hematologist and physician-scientist with expertise in hematologic pathology and clinical laboratory medicine. His research investigates how clonal evolution in conditions like PNH shapes disease trajectory.

Dr. Hsia's work spans the interface of clinical hematology and laboratory science ‚Äî studying how changes in laboratory parameters over time relate to clinical outcomes, and building longitudinal research programs that link lab findings with patient trajectories.

He brings deep expertise in flow cytometry, morphology, and hematologic diagnosis, and plays a central role in the lab's translational research agenda.
